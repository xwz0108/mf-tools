## Understanding Color Blindness

Color blindness affects 1 in 12 men and 1 in 200 women. It is not "seeing in black and white" — it is difficulty distinguishing certain colors.

### Types of Color Blindness

| Type | Affected Colors | Prevalence |
|------|----------------|:--:|
| Deuteranomaly | Red-green (green weak) | 5% of males |
| Protanomaly | Red-green (red weak) | 1% of males |
| Tritanomaly | Blue-yellow | Rare |
| Monochromacy | No color vision | Very rare |

### Why It Matters for Designers

- **8% of male users** cannot distinguish red and green
- Red error messages may be invisible
- Color-only indicators fail accessibility
- **WCAG requires** information not rely solely on color

### Designing for Color Blindness

1. Don't rely on color alone — add icons, patterns, or text
2. Test with a color blindness simulator
3. Use high contrast ratios
4. Avoid red-green combinations for important info
5. Check blue text on dark backgrounds
