## Notion vs Obsidian vs Anytype: Note App Comparison 2026

Three note-taking philosophies compared. Which one fits your workflow?

### The Core Difference

| | Notion | Obsidian | Anytype |
|--|--------|----------|---------|
| Philosophy | All-in-one workspace | Local-first knowledge graph | Local-first with sync |
| Data location | Cloud | Your device | Your device + optional sync |
| Offline | Limited | Full | Full |
| Free tier | Generous | Full (sync extra) | Full |
| Extensibility | API + integrations | 1000+ plugins | Limited |

### Notion — Best for Teams

Notion is a workspace, not just a note app. Databases, kanban boards, wikis, project tracking — all in one place. Best for teams that need collaboration and structure.

### Obsidian — Best for Deep Thinkers

Obsidian stores notes as local Markdown files. You own your data. The graph view shows connections between ideas. Best for researchers, writers, and anyone building a personal knowledge base.

### Anytype — Best of Both Worlds

Anytype combines Notion's structure with Obsidian's local-first philosophy. Notes are stored locally and can sync across devices. Still maturing but promising.

### Verdict

Teams → Notion. Individuals → Obsidian. Want both → try Anytype.
