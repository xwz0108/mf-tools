## How QR Codes Work

QR (Quick Response) codes are 2D barcodes that store data in a grid of black and white squares.

### Anatomy of a QR Code

1. **Finder patterns** — Three squares in corners for alignment
2. **Timing patterns** — Alternating dots for grid reference
3. **Format info** — Error correction level and mask pattern
4. **Data area** — The actual encoded content

### Error Correction Levels

| Level | Recovery | Use Case |
|-------|:--------:|----------|
| L | 7% | Clean environment |
| M | 15% | General use |
| Q | 25% | Industrial |
| H | 30% | Damaged/artistic |

### What Can QR Codes Store?

- URLs (most common)
- Plain text
- Contact information (vCard)
- Wi-Fi credentials
- Payment information

### Size vs. Data Capacity
More data = bigger QR code = harder to scan. Keep URLs short for best results.
